El Niño Phenomenon

How Pacific sea-surface warming changes rainfall, monsoon, and seasonal weather risks.

El Niño is a recurring warming of the central and eastern tropical Pacific. It shifts atmospheric circulation and can change weather risks far beyond the Pacific basin.

Signals to track

  • Niño 3.4 sea-surface temperature anomalies.
  • Trade wind weakening and convection moving eastward.
  • Regional rainfall and temperature departures.
