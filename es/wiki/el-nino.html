What is El Niño?

El Niño is a warming of the central and eastern tropical Pacific Ocean. It shifts rainfall patterns, influences monsoons, and can raise global average temperatures for months at a time.

How it affects weather

  • Wetter-than-average winters in the southern United States and drier conditions in Australia and Indonesia.
  • Weaker Indian monsoon rains in many El Niño years.
  • Warmer global mean temperatures due to heat release from the Pacific.

What to watch

  • Sea-surface temperature anomalies in the Niño 3.4 region.
  • Atmospheric response, including weaker trade winds and shifting jet streams.
  • Local forecasts, because global patterns do not determine every daily outcome.
