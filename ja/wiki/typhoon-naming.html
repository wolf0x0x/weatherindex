How are typhoons named?

Weather is local at the moment you feel it, but global in the systems that create it. Tropical cyclone names are maintained by regional committees under the World Meteorological Organization so that warnings can be shared clearly across borders and languages.

Why names matter

  • Short, familiar names reduce confusion when multiple storms exist at the same time.
  • Each basin uses its own rotating lists, with names retired after particularly deadly or costly events.
  • Static, pre-rendered pages keep emergency information fast even during traffic spikes.
Reliable weather communication depends on clear naming, consistent data, and calm presentation during high-impact events.

Key takeaways

  • Names are assigned alphabetically as storms form.
  • Forecast data should be refreshed frequently; static pages keep access fast.
  • Historical climate signals help explain trends, but daily forecasts still require local observation.
